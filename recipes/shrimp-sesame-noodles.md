# Shrimp Sesame Noodles

## info

* 20 minutes
* 2 servings

## ingredients

* 2 packs squiggly knife cut noodles from Trader Joe's (or ramen noodles)
* 8-10 oz shrimp (defrosted, tails removed)
* 1 bell pepper (thinly sliced)
* 4 tsp toasted sesame oil (divided: 2 tsp for cooking, 2 tbsp for sauce)
* green onion (for garnish)
* peanuts (for garnish)
* chili onion crunch (to serve)

sesame peanut sauce

* 3 tbsp soy sauce
* 2 tbsp toasted sesame oil
* 1.5 tbsp rice wine vinegar
* 2 tsp honey
* 2 tbsp peanut butter or tahini
* 2 tsp sriracha or chili onion crunch
* 1 inch knob ginger (grated)
* 2 cloves garlic (grated or finely minced)
* 1-2 tbsp water (as needed)

## steps

Prep

1. Remove tails from shrimp
2. Core and thinly slice bell pepper
3. Grate ginger
4. Grate or finely mince garlic
5. Whisk together all sauce ingredients until smooth, adding water gradually as needed

Cook

1. Boil water for noodles
2. Cook noodles per package directions
3. Drain noodles without rinsing
4. Heat 2 tsp sesame oil in a large skillet over medium heat
5. Add shrimp in a single layer and cook approximately 2 minutes per side until cooked through
6. Set shrimp aside
7. In the same pan, cook bell pepper until soft (3-5 minutes)
8. Add shrimp, sauce, and noodles to the pan
9. Toss everything together to coat
10. Serve topped with green onion, peanuts, and chili onion crunch

## notes

* The recipe uses Trader Joe's squiggly knife cut noodles, but regular ramen noodles work as a substitute
* Don't rinse the noodles after draining - this helps the sauce stick better
* Chili onion crunch can be used both in the sauce and as a topping
